     St. Croix Marsh Body Boundary Variation Order
     made under Section 13 of the
     Agricultural Marshland Conservation Act
     S.N.S. 2000, c. 22
     N.S. Reg. 50/2009 (February 24, 2009)

            Whereas the St. Croix Marsh Body was incorporated on June 21,
     1950;

            And whereas the lands described in the Certificate of
     Incorporation for the St. Croix Marsh Body are shown in the Plan of
     Works St. Croix NS 38/6, Sheets 1 and 2 of 2;

            And whereas the Agricultural Marshland Conservation Commission
     recommends that the boundary of the marshland section for the St.
     Croix Marsh Body is incorporated be varied so as to exclude from the
     marshland section Lots 17 and 19 on Plan NS 38/6, Sheets 1 and 2 of 2
     as shown on the Plan of Works St. Croix, attached hereto as Schedule
     ``A'';

            And whereas a resolution requesting the variation was passed
     at a special meeting of the members of the St. Croix Marsh Body by a
     vote of not fewer than two thirds of the members of the St. Croix
     Marsh Body present at the meeting and the owners of not less than two
     thirds of the marshland proposed to be excluded favour of the
     variation; 

            Therefore, I, Mark Parent, Minister of Agriculture, pursuant
     to Section 13 of Chapter 22 of the Acts of 2000, the Agricultural
     Marshland Conservation Act, vary the boundary of the marshland
     section for which the St. Croix Marsh Body is incorporated by
     excluding from the marshland section Lots 17 and 19 on Plan NS 38/6,
     Sheets 1 and 2 of 2 as shown on the Plan of Works St. Croix attached
     hereto as Schedule ``A'';

            In accordance with subsection 13(3), the following is a
     description of the area to be excluded from the marshland section for
     which the St. Croix Marsh Body is incorporated:

            All that certain piece and parcel of dykeland shown on Plan NS
     38/6, Sheets 1 and 2 of 2 of Plan of Works St. Croix containing Lot
     17, starting at an iron pin on the north end of the dyke then
     following the tidal side of the dyke right-of-way to an iron pin on
     the south end of the dyke, then following the high water mark at
     elevation 26.0 feet to place of beginning; and

            All that certain piece and parcel of dykeland shown on Plan NS
     38/6, Sheets 1 and 2 of 2 of Plan of Works St. Croix containing Lot
     19, starting at an iron pin on the west end of the dyke then
     following the tidal side of the dyke right-of-way to an iron pin on
     the east end of the dyke, then following the high water mark at
     elevation 26.0 feet to place of beginning.

                                                                              Sgd.:
     Mark Parent
                                                                              The
     Honourable Mark Parent
                                                                              Minister
     of Agriculture

     Halifax, Nova Scotia
     24 Feb, 2009

     Resolution of the St. Croix Marsh Body
     Made at a Special Meeting held
     the 27th day of August, A.D., 2008

            Whereas it is the intention of not fewer than two-thirds of
     the members of the NS 38
     St. Croix Marsh Body that the boundaries of the St. Croix Marsh
     marshland section described in the Certificate of Incorporation of
     the NS 38 St. Croix Marsh Body be varied to delete as marshland the
     lands described in the Certification [Certificate] of Incorporation
     as Lot 17 George Spence and Lot 19 CL/PL Smith for the St. Croix
     Marsh marshland section;

            Be it resolved that the Minister of Agriculture of the
     Province of Nova Scotia is respectfully requested to approve a
     variation of the boundaries of the St. Croix Marsh marshland section
     to exclude as marshland the lands described as Lots 17 and 19 in the
     Certificate of Incorporation for the NS 38 St. Croix Marsh Body.
      
     Certified to be a true copy of a resolution passed by 66 percent of
     the members of the NS 38 St. Croix Marsh Body owning land within the
     boundaries of the St. Croix Marsh marshland section who are resident
     in the province of Nova Scotia and who own not less than percent of
     the marshland within the St. Croix Marsh marshland section [sic].
      
     Sgd.: Ed Cochran
     Chair
     Sgd.: Charlotte Harper
     Secretary
